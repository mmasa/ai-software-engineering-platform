# ASEP Constitution

The Constitution states the enduring principles of the AI Software Engineering
Platform. It changes rarely and only by deliberate amendment. Where any other
document conflicts with the Constitution, the Constitution prevails.

## Articles

### Article 1 — Purpose
ASEP exists to generate, upgrade, convert, assess, and govern software
engineering projects in an AI-native, human-governed, evidence-based way.

### Article 2 — Canonical Language
English is the canonical semantic language. All logic, identifiers, and schema
keys are expressed in English. Localized content is a generated artifact and is
never used for program logic.

### Article 3 — Source of Truth
Structured schemas are the source of truth. Human-facing documentation is
generated from canonical models wherever possible.

### Article 4 — Stable Identity
Every important concept carries a stable, language-independent identifier.
Identifiers survive regeneration and are never reused for a different concept.

### Article 5 — Human Governance
AI performs the work; humans retain authority. Every change is reviewable and
flows through pull requests. Automated actions are auditable.

### Article 6 — Evidence
Assurance is evidence-based. Claims of compliance or quality are backed by
Evidence records linked to Controls.

### Article 7 — Safety by Default
The platform is safe by default. It holds no secrets, performs no destructive
action implicitly, and enables external side effects only when explicitly
configured.

### Article 8 — Modularity and Extensibility
The platform is modular, loosely coupled, and extensible, designed to be
maintained for many years and to accept future engines, profiles, languages,
and plugins without redesign.

## Amendment
Amendments are proposed by pull request, recorded, and — when they change an
architectural decision — accompanied by an Architecture Decision Record (ADR).
